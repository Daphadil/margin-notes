At Home

Housewarming

by Mara  ·  6 readers

Petra came by on Saturday. I invited her to see the apartment. It felt like the right time — everything is in its place now. I have been here long enough to know where everything belongs.

She brought wine. I had already put two glasses on the table. She noticed that and smiled and said I was always prepared. I said yes. I am always prepared now.

I showed her the shelf. Gerald. The books. The mirror, which I turned back to face the room last week — it felt correct to do that. Petra looked at it for a moment longer than the other things. She didn't say anything about it. I didn't ask.

She asked if I was sleeping well. I told her better than ever. She said I seemed different. I asked if that was good. She said she wasn't sure yet and then laughed like it was a joke. I laughed too, at the right moment.

Petra and me. The apartment is finally finished.

It was a good evening. Petra stayed for two hours and then said she had an early morning. I walked her to the door. She paused in the hallway and looked back once, at the ceiling. I waited. She left.

The apartment was quiet after. The good kind of quiet. Everything exactly where it should be. I stood in the middle of the living room for a while and looked at all of it — the shelf, Gerald, the mirror, the ceiling — and felt something that I think is satisfaction.
