Still Here

June

by Mara  ·  6 readers

Hi. I know it's been a while. I don't have a good explanation for that — or I do, but it's not very interesting, and I don't want to make a whole thing of it. I'm fine. Everything is fine. Summer came and I got busy and I forgot to write, the way you forget to call someone and then enough time passes that the call feels too big to make. Anyway. I'm making it.

Gerald is doing incredibly well, actually. Like suspiciously well. I don't know what I've been doing right but he's put out at least four new leaves since March. I keep meaning to photograph him to show the progress. I will. I keep meaning to.

living room, afternoon. I've been looking up a lot lately.

The coffee shop was still there. I went every morning, same corner table, same order. It felt important to keep doing that — the routine of it, the proof that I was still someone who had routines. The barista learned my name. I remember thinking that was enough.

The apartment is quieter in summer. The radiator stopped doing its thing. I don't miss it exactly, but I notice the absence. It's strange, how you adjust to a sound so completely that silence becomes the thing that wakes you up.

I've been looking at the ceiling a lot. I don't know when that started.

Gerald keeps growing. I check on him every morning. I don't know what I'm checking for.

There's nothing on the shelf where he sits. I see it clearly in the mirror. But when I walk over he's there, leaves and all, soil still damp. I stopped trying to figure out which one is true.

You should have noticed the ceiling sooner.

You thought the building was just breathing. That's what you said. That's the word you used.

The plant was already gone by then. You just couldn't see it yet.

I think I'm writing this so you'll know what to look for. The shadow doesn't come from the light. It doesn't come from anything you can name. But it knew you were here before you did, and I think — I think it was waiting to see if you'd stay.

You stayed.

I'm sorry. I would have told you not to.
