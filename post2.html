Week One

Getting Settled

by Mara  ·  4 min read  ·  6 readers

A week in. The boxes are almost gone. I found a coffee shop four minutes from here that plays music quietly enough that you can actually think, which I consider a moral achievement on their part. I've been there every morning.

I bought a plant — a small pothos, very hard to kill apparently, which is the only kind of plant I'm qualified to own. I put it on the shelf in the living room next to a few books and the little oval mirror I've had since college. It looked nice enough that I took a photo.

the shelf. the plant is named Gerald provisionally.

The building is noisier at night than I expected. Not loud — more like it breathes. Radiator ticks, the elevator hum, something that might be pipes or might be upstairs neighbors doing whatever upstairs neighbors do at 2am. I don't mind. City apartments are supposed to sound like something.

I called my mom on Tuesday. She asked if I'd met anyone yet and I said yes, a neighbor named Petra who gave me honey. My mom said "that's not what I meant" and I said "I know." Standard Tuesday.

I've been sleeping well, actually. Better than I did in the old place. I don't remember my dreаms, which is new for me — I used to always remember them.think I sleep at all. I think I just stop, and then start again. I'm choosing to see that as progress.

Anyway. Week one: fine. Better than fine. Quiet, mostly.
