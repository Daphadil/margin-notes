Moving On

Wrapping Up

by Mara  ·  6 readers

I've been thinking for a while now about whether to keep writing here. It started as a place to put small things down, the way you put keys on a hook by the door — just so you know where they are. But I don't think I need that anymore. I know where everything is.

The apartment is exactly how I want it. I stopped moving things around, which I used to do constantly in the first weeks — restless, adjusting, never quite right. Now everything is right. I walk through the rooms and they feel correct. I don't have another word for it. Correct.

I've been spending more time with Petra. She came by twice this week. She seems comfortable here now — she stays longer each visit, asks fewer questions. Last time she sat on the floor next to the shelf and didn't say anything for a while, and that was fine. It was good, actually. I think she's settling in too.

I went through the old posts this morning. There are some from the beginning that don't sound like me — too uncertain, too searching. I'm going to take them down. And then I think I'll take the rest down too. There's no reason to leave them up. They were for a version of this that I don't need to document anymore.

Note from the author This blog will be set to private in the next few days. Thank you to everyone who read. It meant something, to know someone was here.

I think that's it. I think I'm done. The apartment is quiet. Gerald is on the shelf. The light comes in from the west in the afternoon and turns everything the same color, the way it always has, and I stand in it for a while every day and feel nothing missing.

That's the right feeling. Nothing missing.
