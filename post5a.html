Still Here

Outside

by Mara  ·  6 readers

I don't know how to write this. I've started it four times. The other three are deleted — they were too long, too explanatory, too much like I was trying to convince myself something had happened when I'm not sure I'm ready to say what happened.

So I'm going to write it small.

I left the apartment this morning with one bag and Gerald.

I am writing this from a coffee shop I've never been to before, in a neighborhood I don't know.

The light here is different. I keep noticing that. I don't know yet if that's good.

I'm not going to explain the last few months. Not here, not yet. Maybe not ever in a way that would make sense written down. There are things that happened that I understand and things that happened that I don't, and I've decided it's okay to hold both of those at the same time without resolving them.

What I know: I heard something. I wrote something, or something wrote it through me. And then I left.

Gerald is on the table next to my coffee. He looks the same. He looks fine. I've been checking on him every few minutes like he might disappear, but he's just sitting there putting out leaves the way he always has, not knowing anything is different. I think that's the right approach.

I've been thinking about something I wrote a while ago. I said I was writing it so you'd know what to look for.

I think you found it. I think something found it for you.

You asked me once — you didn't write it down, but you thought it, I remember — whether the building was just breathing. That's the word you used. Breathing.

It wasn't. But you already know that now.

Take the plant. Don't look at the ceiling. Walk out the front door and don't count your steps and don't look back at the window, even if you think you see something.

You stayed too long. I know. So did I. But we're outside now, and the coffee here is good, and the light is different, and that's enough for today.

That's enough.
