Moving Day

New Place

by Mara  ·  3 min read  ·  6 readers

I'm writing this from the floor of my new living room because my desk chair is somewhere inside the big box labeled misc office things and I don't have the energy to find it. There are eleven boxes total. I labeled eight of them. The other three are a mystery even to me.

The apartment is on the fourth floor of an old building — pre-war, the listing said, which I've learned means "the radiators make noise and the windows stick." Both true. But the ceilings are high and the light in the afternoon comes in from the west and turns everything the color of tea, and I think I'm going to like it here.

click to close
the living room, ~4pm. eleven boxes. (not pictured: the radiator, which is already being loud)

My neighbor knocked this evening to introduce herself. Her name is Petra, she's lived here twelve years, and she brought a small jar of honey from what she described as "a friend's hives upstate." She seemed surprised when I opened the door — just for a second, before she smiled. I don't know what she was expecting.

I made tea and drank it standing up and watched the city do its evening thing through the stuck window. It all felt very ordinary.I don't remember deciding to move here.

Anyway. I start unpacking tomorrow. If you're reading this — hi, I'm Mara, this is my blog, I write about nothing in particular. I've had it for three years and I don't think anyone reads it and that's fine. It's more like a diary that I don't bother to lock.
